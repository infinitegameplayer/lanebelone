<p></p><figure><div><img src="//img1.wsimg.com/isteam/ip/63b44413-1bd1-41f7-a1a3-d748eba816d5/Lane%20Belone%20Mission.png/:/cr=t:0%25,l:0%25,w:100%25,h:100%25/rs=w:1280"></div></figure><p></p><p>I spent 12 years in the military, mission is in my blood.</p><p>A big chunk of hardship when I got out was a lack of mission. In the military, it's easy to put focus and energy towards the mission.</p><p>When you get out, you have to either find a new mission that someone else has created, or you find your own.</p><p>I knew that I needed to find my own and it's a much more challenge path.</p><p>Over the past few years, I've honed in on my purpose, play and peace. And feel in a great spot to finally consolidate my life and form this Mission.</p><p>If you haven't checked out my Vision, <a href="https://lanebelone.com/blog/f/lane-belone-vision" rel="noopener" target="_blank">please check it out here</a>.</p><p>A Vision is what you want to see in the world, and the Mission is the action you can do to get there.</p><p>Each sentence has a slightly different focus, but all fit together like peanut butter and jelly. The harmoniously form an ecosystem for me to play in.</p><p>To be sure, this will evolve over time, but it is the best I have at this point in time.</p><p>When I wrote (typed) out my Vision, it just flowed out in about 8 seconds. My Mission was a little different.</p><p>Over the course of the day, I chewed on it a bit and made some changes. The next day I felt the calling to "publish" it out in the world.</p><p><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/lanebelone/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">My first stop was to update my "About" section in LinkedIn</a>. Yeah I know, kinda funny. I initially was only thinking it would be an unofficially Vision and Mission statement.</p><p>Then as I copied and Vision and Mission statement, I felt there was something missing.</p><p>So I tapped in the moment and felt what else wanted to be said. Because I was on LinkedIn, I felt there was something missing about "business stuff". So that's when I added the second sentence.</p><p>Then I had it.</p><p>I felt good about it. It read the following.</p><p><em><strong>"Express a Joyful, Harmonious and Sovereign message as the moment calls for. Advise leaders, business owners and creatives towards Joyful Sovereignty. Connect unlikely people and communities to share wisdom and spark genuine empowerment. Harmonize collaborative efforts with joy and wisdom. Co-Create projects, programs, products, courses and experiences as the moment calls for."</strong></em></p><p>Even though a part of me felt like it could be more complete. I rolled with it.</p><p>And by taking imperfect action, I updated the sentence that was missing. That's what I've been learning deeply recently. To take a step and watch the next insight unfold right before my eyes without really even trying.</p><p>It's a beautiful thing, but requires a deep level of trust.</p><p>Trust in myself.</p><p>Trust in life itself.</p><p>So the journey continues...</p>
