Something has been pulling tight lately. You can probably feel it too.

The feeds, the headlines, the way a single scroll can send fiery energy rushing through your body. People you love are certain about opposite things. The sense that everyone is braced for something nobody can quite name. It lives in the body long before it ever becomes an argument.

I've been noticing my own small version of it too. For a long time I made things to get somewhere. To prove something, to earn something, to arrive. Lately I catch myself making things because it's fun. Because I'm alive. Because the morning seemed to ask for it. The difference is quiet and it changes everything. One comes from pressure. The other comes from play.

And I keep wondering whether what we're all feeling isn't the end of something. Maybe it's a dance. A long, awkward, beautiful dance from one way of living into another, happening to all of us at the same time. A few days ago I wrote about that dance up close, [the personal one](https://www.lanebelone.com/blog/f/the-dance-youre-already-in). This is the same dance, zoomed out.

Here's the thing about a dance between paradigms. The old way doesn't leave because you decided it should. It lingers in the body like an old friend.

These "old friends" aren't villains. They're [the conditioning we grew up inside](https://www.infinitegameos.io/concepts/conditioned-vs-authentic-identity). The job you tolerate because money can feel like a hostage situation. The quiet voice that says this is just how it works, this is just how it's always been. We learned them young, for good reasons. They kept us safe for a while.

The loudest old friend right now wears a costume. It looks like the fight. The endless, exhausting brawl where one side is certain it's right and the other side is just as certain. Underneath the costumes, both sides are saying the same sentence and feeling the same fear. The brawl keeps everyone swinging sideways so nobody has a moment to pause and look up. That's the whole trick of it. It pins unsuspecting people against each other and keeps the room too loud for anyone to hear the music.

You can't win that fight. It was never built to be won. It's built to keep going.

So the move isn't to swing harder or pick a better side. The move is quieter than that. It's to notice that the question you're being handed might be the trap. To feel the pull to take a side, and pause, and wonder whether there's a larger floor underneath the whole ring.

There is. There always was. It's the oldest game there is, the Infinite Game, the one that was never built to end. The fight is just a small loud room inside a very large house.

When you step off the board, the "new friends" are already there. They've been arriving the whole time, quietly, waiting for you to turn around.

A new friend is the option you didn't think you had before. Work that comes from play instead of pressure. The voice that's actually yours instead of the one your side handed you. The pause before the swing. The calm that turns out to be a stance and not a luxury. The willingness to be easy on each other, the way you'd want someone to be easy on you on a hard day.

None of it asks you to win anything. It only asks you to turn toward the new and let the old friends rest. They did their work. They can retire now. We get to thank them and keep walking.

The path won't be clear. It weaves. The fog rolls in and the next step disappears for a while. That's normal. You don't need the whole map. You need a compass, a direction you trust and one honest step. The rest shows itself as you walk.

This is the dance we're all in now. Not a campaign, not a movement, nothing to sign. Just a turning, one person at a time, toward a way of living that was always available underneath the noise.

You're already on the floor. You have been the whole time. The music is playing. The path is lit (not that kind of lit lol) just enough to see the next step. The valley ahead is wider than the fight ever let us believe. Be easy on each other. Keep walking.
