<p></p><figure><div><img src="//img1.wsimg.com/isteam/ip/63b44413-1bd1-41f7-a1a3-d748eba816d5/Archetypal%20Expressions%20-%20Joyful%20King.png/:/cr=t:0%25,l:0%25,w:100%25,h:100%25/rs=w:1280"></div></figure><p></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>What your Heart has been saying, may not be what you think.</p><p>For many years, I felt I had to earn my worth to get what I desired. It would drive my behavior to achieve, and THEN I would be able to get what I “felt I wanted”.</p><p>When in reality my Heart was just radiating who I already was. I just didn’t have the capacity to understand what was happening.</p><p>My heart was telling me who I already was, but it was my mind that was limiting on who I could Be and what I could experience. I’m grateful my Heart continued to share my own truth the entire time, even when I didn’t feel it all the way.</p><p>You are worthy of receiving everything in your heart. Your heart isn’t really telling you what you deserve or want, it’s actually a beautiful reminder of what you already have, who you already are. Yet you’ve learned from others and made it your own to hold back and limit what you experience and receive.</p><p>The problem is, your amnesia has deeply effected your ability to remember who you truly are and your worth. You’ve identified as someone or something that has to do something in order to receive what you already are.</p><p>Once you remember who you truly are, you dissolve the barrier to receive. Your true Self emerges more each day. You experience those whispers your heart has been telling you about all these years.</p><p>“Not true,” a part of you says.</p><p>Consider experimenting with the following:</p><p></p><ol>
  <li>Ask yourself: “If my Heart was actually just telling me who I already am, how would that change my perspective?”</li>
  <li>Get still and quiet</li>
  <li>Get extra still and quiet</li>
  <li>Listen</li>
  <li>Write down the answer that comes</li>
</ol><p></p><p>What you decide to do with your answer, is up to you.</p><p>What message from your Heart have you misinterpreted in the past? How did it feel to understand it after the fact?</p>
