<p></p><figure><div><img alt="" src="//img1.wsimg.com/isteam/ip/63b44413-1bd1-41f7-a1a3-d748eba816d5/The%20Distillation%20%2320%20-%20Synergy.png/:/cr=t:0%25,l:0%25,w:100%25,h:100%25/rs=w:1280"></div></figure><p></p><p>&nbsp;</p><div><h2><em><strong>My Intention</strong></em></h2></div><p><em>For this 20th edition of The Distillation, my intention is to bring a deeper awareness of Synergy and how to apply it to your life.</em></p><p></p><hr><p></p><p>For years, I’ve been unconsciously navigating letting go of control.</p><p><br>
The past several months I’ve contemplated, “where in my life am I controlling?”</p><p><br>
To even ask that question, was big growth for me.</p><p><br>
Because I had stories that I wasn’t overly controlling.</p><p><br>
When I look at my life, my relationships are approached with harmony.</p><p><br>
So I told myself that I didn’t have other areas where I was controlling.</p><p><br>
Yet, as I contemplated with sincere curiosity, I soon found my answer.</p><p><br>
I began to notice when I had a “good idea” I wanted to explore, I’d immediately develop a plan and gain certainty on that path.</p><p><br>
I was fearful of an uncertain trajectory, so I often strategized so I could feel more comfortable about moving forward.</p><p><br>
This pattern would happen swiftly, automatically and without thought.</p><p><br>
So as this clarity emerged, it made way for a simple, yet powerful concept.</p><p><br>
I soon fell into a word that I was somewhat familiar, but I hadn’t heard it explained in this way.</p><p><br>
That word was Synergy.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Synergy</strong></h2></div><p>For me, synergy was related with the idea of mixing some stuff together in harmony.</p><p><br>
But I soon found a key within synergy that unlocked something meaningful to me.</p><p><br>
I was exploring the <a href="https://genekeys.com/" rel="">Gene Keys</a> and the way Richard Rudd explained synergy, something clicked for me.</p><p><br>
That was: “self-organizing”.</p><p><br>
It meant I didn't have to do all the work.</p><p><br>
It meant that I was able to take a step back and steer my course with greater clarity and ease.</p><p><br>
It meant I could navigate uncertainty with greater peace.</p><p><br>
<u>Here’s a working definition of Synergy:</u><br>
</p><p></p><blockquote><em><strong>To bring together self-organizing energies to collaborate, complement and co-create in a harmonious, empowered and strategic way.</strong></em></blockquote><p></p><p>This process involves leveraging diverse strengths and perspectives to produce outcomes that are greater than the sum of individual efforts.</p><p><br>
This fosters an environment where collaboration enhances innovation, efficiency and effectiveness.</p><p><br>
Where it really took off (for me), is when I applied this concept to archetypal energies (which we’ll dive into in another Distillation).</p><p><br>
Okay, all that sounds cool, but how do we apply it to life?</p><p><br>
Before getting into that, let’s discuss how synergy helps surrender control.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Surrendering Control</strong></h2></div><p>Little did I know, synergy was the mental nugget I needed to let go of control on a deeper level.</p><p><br>
It gave me the peace of mind to allow other aspects of myself to operate intelligently.</p><p><br>
It allowed me to trust myself, aspects of myself and Life itself more.</p><p><br>
This surrendering gave me deeper state of peace.</p><p><br>
The shift was allowing aspects of myself to simply be themselves and do what they do.</p><p><br>
I soon noticed that synergy was everywhere, I’d just been blind to it.</p><p><br>
Synergy is also a great concept to bring into leadership.</p><p><br>
Because everyone knows how unhelpful a micro-manager is lol. Synergy alleviates that issue.</p><p><br>
Surrendering control of the granular, allows you to zoom out and see the bigger picture.</p><p><br>
That bigger picture brings clarity and the trajectory you decide to embark, becomes simple.</p><p><br>
By aligning intentions and actions, synergy creates a dynamic interplay that maximizes potential and drives collective success.</p><p><br>
Below, I’ll share 3 areas to understand this concept a bit more. Then we’ll finish with some action steps (like we always do).</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>The Body</strong></h2></div><p>There is a <a href="https://www.mindbodygreen.com/articles/7-hermetic-principles" rel="">Hermetic</a> Law of Correspondence which states, “As above, so below. As within, so without.”</p><p><br>
Basically, it means that what is internal, is reflected in the external (and vice versa).</p><p><br>
So let’s begin with the body (internal).</p><p><br>
Understanding within yourself, makes way for easier implementation externally.</p><p><br>
As you know, the body has many systems that run on their own.</p><p><br>
Your digestion system.</p><p><br>
Your nervous system.</p><p><br>
Your respiratory system.</p><p><br>
All these systems are self-organized and operate without you thinking about it.</p><p><br>
Our bodies are the perfect example of many systems operating in synergy.</p><p><br>
Often times, our minds can worry and experience thoughts that bring the harmony of our body out of sync.</p><p><br>
Essentially, our mind is trying to do the job of the body. Which isn’t what the mind was designed to do.</p><p><br>
By connecting with the body, we attune ourselves to effortlessly listen to and understand its needs.</p><p><br>
The part of us that is worried gets out of the way and simply allows our body’s systems to operate in the best and most efficient way.</p><p><br>
As a result, you receive more self-awareness, self-leadership and self-mastery.</p><p><br>
This practical application lends itself to being able to express this concept of synergy in other areas of life.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Family Dynamic</strong></h2></div><p>Family relationships can be tough sometimes.</p><p><br>
Grounding the concept of synergy into the family can lead to profound (positive) shifts.</p><p><br>
As a Family Leader, empowering each person in your life to authentically express themselves can be one of the most powerful thing you can do.</p><p><br>
By sincerely listening and communicating, you create the conditions for synergy to weave within the dynamic.</p><p><br>
Just as you practiced listening to your body (internally), you reflect (externally) that same practice with your loved ones.</p><p><br>
Eventually, the practice with one, will strengthen the other (and vice versa).</p><p><br>
When we empower others in our family with synergy, we allow them to expand and self-organize within themselves with greater ease.</p><p><br>
They are on the receiving end of synergy, and then that external behavior reflects inwardly for them.</p><p><br>
Then they can more effortlessly practice synergy within their body (like you did).</p><p><br>
As with anything, take this step-by-step, day-by-day.</p><p><br>
The practice of synergy works best when complemented with what you know already works for your family.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Vocation</strong></h2></div><p>Another area we can bring synergy into, is our work.</p><p><br>
In fact, it may be even easier to integrate in this area of your life.</p><p><br>
That’s because the workplace has more defined structure and purpose.</p><p><br>
A good company articulates a vision and mission for the leadership, team members and the customers they serve.</p><p><br>
This clarity allows for individuals to innovate solutions because they know where the company is heading.</p><p><br>
Micro-management can be a pain, so empowering people within your organization brings about higher productivity, creativity and deliverables.</p><p><br>
Imagine you’re the leader of several people in your company.</p><p><br>
How much easier would it be if they operated more intelligently, collaborated efficiently and self-organized their domain of mastery without you having to control it?</p><p><br>
You could put more energy and focus on other areas that you simply haven’t had the time to do before.</p><p><br>
You could also cultivate an environment where creativity, flow and expansion become second nature.</p><p><br>
<strong>Note:</strong> Embracing humility and working on synergy within yourself first, will bring about better results.</p><p><br>
Embody synergy, and then it’s expressed effortlessly as a byproduct.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Action Steps</strong></h2></div><p>Synergy is a deep topic lol.</p><p><br>
It will take weeks and months for it to sink in. So give yourself time and space to allow synergy to digest within you.</p><p><br>
Having said that, let’s dive into the action steps:</p><p></p><ol>
  <li>Within the next 3 days, find some time (when it’s cool) to take a short walk.</li>
  <li>Contemplate this concept of synergy, and ask yourself how it shows up in your body and life.</li>
  <li>After your walk, jot down any ideas that feel important to capture.</li>
  <li>Return to this topic a few days later.</li>
