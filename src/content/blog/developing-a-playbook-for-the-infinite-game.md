Imagine you’ve been a professional treasure hunter for decades.</p><p><br>
Your own version of Indiana Jones.</p><p><br>
Ever since you were a kid, you loved the thrill of finding treasure and hidden secrets.</p><p><br>
For most of your treasure hunts, they were solo adventures.</p><p><br>
No one really understood the game you were playing.</p><p><br>
Until one day, you met someone who played a similar game of treasure hunt than you. But somehow their version was slightly deeper.</p><p><br>
You converse with this other treasure hunter.</p><p><br>
You pick their brain.</p><p><br>
You eventually find yourself on a “mini treasure hunt” with them.</p><p><br>
They ask you a lot of questions on how your treasure hunts in the past have gone.</p><p><br>
They ask of your motivations behind your adventures.</p><p><br>
At the end of the mini treasure hunt, they hand you an envelope with a symbol on the outside.</p><p><br>
They invite you to take “treasure hunting” to the next level. If interested, open it and follow the instructions.</p><p><br>
What do you do?</p><p><br>

</p><div><h2><strong>The Game Most Are Playing</strong></h2></div><p>Most people in the world are playing someone else’s game.</p><p><br>
Even worse, most are playing a game that actually takes away from their natural state.</p><p><br>
The typical go to school, get a degree, get a job…</p><p><br>
This path (game) robs us of our innate creativity and vibrancy of life.</p><p><br>
Some people make good money and then find the things that bring them joy because of the resources they have.</p><p><br>
Most aren’t that “lucky”.</p><p><br>
Most aren’t aware of the game they’re playing.</p><p><br>
What’s sad, is they don’t even know how much they’ve given their power away.</p><p><br>
I know, I’m speaking from experience.</p><p><br>
I’ve played a disempowering game in the past. And I’m still playing my way out of that old game and into a “new” game in some ways.</p><p><br>
The first step in being able to play a more infinite game, is to identify that you’ve been playing a more finite game.</p><p><br>
Bring it to your awareness.</p><p><br>
Appreciate it for what it is.</p><p><br>
Decide that you’ll play a more infinite game from here on out.</p><p><br>
You don’t need to know how to play in the beginning. All that’s needed is you deciding that you want to play.</p><p><br>
The HOW emerges as you take each step.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>The Playbook</strong></h2></div><p>The Joyful Sovereignty Infinite Game has a playbook, sort of.</p><p><br>
Imagine you could see every playbook from every NFL, NBA and pro sports team.</p><p><br>
Every foundation, principle, trick play, formation and guideline at your disposal.</p><p><br>
With so many resources and options, you’d need something to know how to pull from what team and playbook.</p><p><br>
You’d need a Golden Thread to weave the perfect combination.</p><p><br>
This combination of plays, foundations, principles, guidelines and even trick plays all fit together harmoniously.</p><p><br>
Here’s the secret.</p><p><br>
YOU are the Golden Thread.</p><p><br>
The authenticity of who you are will automatically attract the right components of each of the playbooks.</p><p><br>
Your curiosity will drive this.</p><p><br>
Your excitement will drive this.</p><p><br>
Your fun will drive this.</p><p><br>
“Playbooks” are all around. Lots of people want to give them to you for free.</p><p><br>
Some people want to sell you their playbook.</p><p><br>
What most are missing, is they think their exact playbook will be perfect for you.<br>
But it won’t be.</p><p><br>
It’s true, there are some universal "plays" that will support.</p><p><br>
Your curated playbook will begin to emerge as you expose yourself to other peoples intentions, actions, question and approaches to life.</p><p><br>
In addition to welcoming inspiration from other peoples playbooks, you will also look within.</p><p><br>
Solitude and stillness will be containers for you to receive plays from a source you may not be able to explain.</p><p><br>
Having a bunch of plays you think are for you is good, but you’ll need to test them out.</p><p><br>
As you test them out, you’ll find out which ones resonate with you.</p><p><br>
All the ones that don’t, discard them and appreciate the wisdom it brought you.</p><p><br>
This infinite game is full spectrum.</p><p><br>
It includes your Mind, Body and Soul.</p><p><br>
Your Body is the vehicle.</p><p><br>
Your Soul is in the drivers seat.</p><p><br>
Having your mind (and what it thinks) is only part of the picture.</p><p><br>
Over time, you’ll develop a custom playbook, fully verified and vetted by yourself.</p><p><br>
It’ll be in alignment with your Mind, Body and Soul.</p><p><br>
Each aspect of you will give it the thumbs up.</p><p><br>
It’ll make sense to you mentally.</p><p><br>
It’ll feel good in your Body.</p><p><br>
And a deep knowing in your Soul will seal the deal.</p><p><br>
This is what is meant by integration.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>What Are The “Plays”?</strong></h2></div><p>If life is an infinite game, let’s dive into what the “plays” might look like.</p><p><br>
Plays are something you decidedly do.</p><p><br>
<u>These come in the form of the following:</u></p><p></p><ul>
  <li>Intentions</li>
  <li>Questions</li>
  <li>Actions</li>
  <li>Approaches</li>
</ul><p></p><p>Just like in any game, if you run a single play, it may work once.</p><p><br>
Then as you continue to play the game, you run a complementary play (for you). Rinse and repeat.</p><p><br>
These different “plays” all complement each other.</p><p><br>
Let’s break each of these kinds of “plays” down.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h3><strong>Intentions</strong></h3></div><p>Getting clear on your intentions are one of the most powerful ways to play.</p><p><br>
Intentions are the spark of how you play the game.</p><p><br>
Intentions often bring the long-term to the game.</p><p><br>
<u>Here are a few examples:</u></p><p></p><ul>
  <li>I intend to prioritize my self-care every day</li>
  <li>It’s my intention to have fun once a week and laugh as hard as I can</li>
  <li>I intend to support all those around me as best I can</li>
  <li>It’s my intention to have my work and play complement each other</li>
</ul><p></p><p>There are so many potent intentions you can bring into your life.</p><p><br>
Over time, you’ll embody these intentions and then can bring new ones into your life.</p><p><br>
Don’t rush it, have fun with them.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h3><strong>Questions</strong></h3></div><p>Great questions lead to great answers.</p><p><br>
Asking yourself “keystone” questions will lead to profound answers that will support your journey.</p><p><br>
<u>A few questions that can be “plays” for you are:</u></p><p></p><ul>
  <li>How can I FEEL as much Joy and Sovereignty on a consistent, daily basis?</li>
  <li>How could I be the happiest and most joyful version of myself?</li>
  <li>How can I live deeper in the Present Moment each day?</li>
  <li>How can my curiosities and excitements in life drive cash flow?</li>
</ul><p></p><p>There are so many great questions out there. As you dedicate time to form great questions for yourself, you’ll be rewarded with great answers.</p><p><br>
Those answers will lead you to people, actions, ideas and so much more.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h3><strong>Actions</strong></h3></div><p>Actions are pretty straightforward.</p><p><br>
Yet sometimes we don’t take them.</p><p><br>
Knowing what to do, and actually doing them are sometimes difficult to connect.</p><p><br>
You take action every day. Taking intentional action is where the good stuff happens.</p><p><br>
Especially the stuff where you know you should take action, but don’t want to.</p><p><br>
These moments will unlock a beautiful aspect of the game of life.</p><p><br>
<u>Some actions you take are:</u></p><p></p><ul>
  <li>Doing the uncomfortable thing, but you know you’ll feel satisfied after</li>
  <li>Doing the “scary” thing, but you’ll level up after</li>
  <li>Take action on the thing you’ve been super curious and intrigued by</li>
  <li>Experiment with a project that has been on the back burner for years</li>
</ul><p></p><p>These examples are meant to be more open-ended for you.</p><p><br>
As action is easily understood and done on a daily basis.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h4><strong>Approaches</strong></h4></div><p>Action is WHAT you do, the approach is HOW you do it.</p><p><br>
In sports, some focus on the offensive side of the game. While others focus more on the defensive side.</p><p><br>
Most have a combination of both, but in a unique mix.</p><p><br>
Your approach to the game of life will be unique to you.</p><p><br>
You may have moments (chapters) where you work hard.</p><p><br>
Some chapters of life will be more smooth.</p><p><br>
Over time, you will know what approach to life best suits you for the moment.</p><p><br>
<u>Some examples of approaches might be:</u></p><p></p><ul>
  <li>I approach life with my unique mix of grit and grace</li>
  <li>I approach life by having fun and experimenting, and seeing what happens</li>
  <li>I approach life deeply strategic and intentional</li>
  <li>I approach life with Joy and Sovereignty</li>
</ul><p></p><p>Understand that life is dynamic. The approach you had early in life may not be as applicable today.</p><p><br>
The great thing: because you lived through it, it’s within you.</p><p><br>
It’s within your experience and can be pulled from as needed.</p><p><br>
I was a Green Beret. Sometimes I need to harness that grit and mission focused energy. I have it when I need it.</p><p><br>
Yet now, I’ve harmonized my approach to life.</p><p><br>
It’s still intense in some ways, but it’s also deeply integrated with the flow of life.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h3><strong>Alignment of Plays</strong></h3></div><p>Setting the intention of having all your plays complement and align can be powerful. If you need to ask yourself how they complement, do so!</p><p><br>
Align intentions with your actions.</p><p><br>
Align your questions with your approaches.</p><p><br>
With your action, you’ll solidify your approach of the game.</p><p><br>
You get the idea.</p><p><br>
You don’t want aspects of your life to be at odds with each other.</p><p><br>
That’ll only bring suffering.</p><p><br>
If you need more inspiration on your “plays”, look above to the previous section (The Playbook).</p><p><br>
Noticing how others play the game can help you point yourself in the right direction.</p><p><br>
<strong>Note:</strong> You aren't copying other people. You're simply allowing others to be a mirror for you.</p><p><br>
Who you are is seen in others.</p><p><br>
Ensure you discern what’s right for you, right now.</p><p><br>
Experiment and validate your own playbook.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>The Game Within The Game</strong></h2></div><p>The secret to playing this game isn’t how you play with others, but the game you play within yourself.</p><p><br>
This is the tiny part of the game that pretty much no one else on the planet will quite understand it like you do.</p><p><br>
It's so nuanced and subtlety different, they would have to walk in your shoes your entire life to “get it”.</p><p><br>
References from when you were 12 &nbsp;years old would give it a tiny bit different meaning.</p><p><br>
That experience you had when you visited that one place refined your approach.</p><p><br>
An influential person who shaped you in a specific way.</p><p><br>
No one in the world could quite get how you play.</p><p><br>
This is the game within the game.</p><p><br>
It’s almost like an inside joke between you and the Universe.</p><p><br>
Reference humor to the max lol.</p><p><br>
You can remember watching a show when you were younger with someone. Something so hilarious happened and you both laughed so hard.</p><p><br>
For the next few weeks and even to this day, you laugh at it.</p><p><br>
But for some reason, no one else seems to laugh quite as hard as you and your friend do.</p><p><br>
This is kind of like that, but except it’s all within yourself.</p><p><br>
Why within yourself?</p><p><br>
Because a lot of people on the planet are playing to please others.</p><p><br>
Even worse, they don’t even know it.</p><p><br>
It’s been hard-wired in their brain.</p><p><br>
Maybe you can look back to a game you played as a kid.</p><p><br>
You got so lost in the game, you didn’t even think about other people.</p><p><br>
You were in deep flow (even though you didn’t know that’s what you were doing) with your game.</p><p><br>
I remember doing this with basketball growing up.</p><p><br>
I played for hours every single day.</p><p><br>
I played at the park with friends.</p><p><br>
But I really enjoyed playing in my backyard.</p><p><br>
I could adjust the rim to like 8-9 feet and dunk for hours.</p><p><br>
I’d practice different dunks I saw from NBA players.</p><p><br>
I’d perfect it.</p><p><br>
I broke down every tiny movement.</p><p><br>
I’d slow down time to understand how I could do it.</p><p><br>
It was a game I played within myself.</p><p><br>
I had all the time in the world to do the same thing over and over.</p><p><br>
If I was playing with someone else, they’d probably get tired of it. They wouldn’t have the same internal game within to shift the nuances of how to do it just how I wanted.</p><p><br>
This one example is part of this inner game.</p><p><br>
How could you extend this concept into multiple aspects of life?</p><p><br>
This is definitely a deeper topic, but we’ll worth digging into.</p><p><br>
Spark a conversation with someone about this to help extract some insight for you.</p><p><br>
</p><div><h2><strong>Beginning To Play</strong></h2></div><p>Do you remember the moment you learned how to play the game of tag?</p><p><br>
Do you remember how you first understood it?</p><p><br>
Probably not exactly.</p><p><br>
Maybe you saw some kids playing on the playground when you were a tiny human.</p><p><br>
Then you saw and felt how much fun these kids were having.</p><p><br>
You were so excited, it didn’t even matter if you didn’t understand how to play.</p><p><br>
You jumped in.</p><p><br>
You DECIDED.</p><p><br>
You probably didn’t get it the first try, but you still went for it.</p><p><br>
As you played more, you got better at it.</p><p><br>
Today, you know how to play. You understand it. It’s part of you.</p><p><br>
You may not play tag as much these days (lol), but you have that within you.</p><p><br>
Work can be a game.</p><p><br>
Fun can be a game.</p><p><br>
Hobbies can be a game.</p><p><br>
Family time can be a game.</p><p><br>
Just because you view things MORE like a game, doesn’t mean that you don’t take it sincerely.</p><p><br>
It’s a game that deeply matters to you, but you don’t have to take it all seriously.</p><p><br>
It’ll be a mix (your mix) of purposeful play.</p><p><br>
Just like playing tag for the first time, just jump right in.</p><p><br>
I’ve done my best to present this article to your mind <em><strong>to give it a try</strong></em>.</p><p><br>
I’ve done my best to provide enough “strategy”.</p><p><br>
You have the opportunity to decide a more playful approach to the infinite game of life.</p><p><br>

Your experience in finding treasure from secret clues, ancient texts and hidden puzzles is not a gift. You know how to lead someone else to find treasure.</p><p><br>
And not just any treasure.</p><p><br>
But treasure of ancient wisdom that will unlock an even deeper game.</p><p><br>
Only the intrigued will find this game fun.</p><p><br>
It requires depth, wisdom and play.</p><p><br>
This game reveals secrets that will unlock human potential.</p><p><br>
But it can only be found by those who want to play this particular infinite game.</p><p><br>
You’ve mastered the intentions of your game.</p><p><br>
You’ve mastered the actions you take.</p><p><br>
You’ve mastered the questions you ask yourself.</p><p><br>
You’ve mastered your approach.</p><p><br>
You’ve define the game within the game.</p><p><br>
