<p></p><figure><div><img src="//img1.wsimg.com/isteam/ip/63b44413-1bd1-41f7-a1a3-d748eba816d5/28ACB65A-9203-45AE-8485-2449EF6DF091.jpeg/:/cr=t:0%25,l:0%25,w:100%25,h:100%25/rs=w:1280" alt="Lane at Jungfraujoch" title="Lane at Jungfraujoch"><figcaption>Lane at Jungfraujoch</figcaption></div></figure><p></p><p>I remember when I got to my first duty station in Germany. I was shy, kept to myself and not confident.</p><p>I was a “good dude”, so it was easy to be a grey man, blend in.</p><p>It wasn’t until I met a Brother on the Path that would elevate me and instill massive confidence within myself.</p><p>He even brought me into his family, felt like a true Brother.</p><p>Eventually I would be brought into the gang in the unit and fit in a little more. And then something happened.</p><p>I felt a calling to Be and do something different. I heard about Special Forces and had no idea what it really was.</p><p>I went for it and got selected to a new Path.</p><p>Damn, just as I was just starting to fit in. I must leave my friends and go do something new I have no idea about.</p><p>I train for Special Forces for 2 years.</p><p>I meet another Brother on the Path.</p><p>He brings me in like family. Because I don’t really fit in, again. See a trend here?</p><p>I was an intel guy wanting to be a hardcore SF guy. On paper, I shouldn’t have been there, yet there I was.</p><p>I eventually earn my Green Beret and move to my next assignment.</p><p>I’m a new guy, first deployment as a Green Beret, I met my team down range.</p><p>I didn’t fit in. I was a guy who showed up in the middle of the deployment. I didn’t train with these guys for years before the tour.</p><p>We didn’t get to know each other and learn to trust each other like it usually happens, especially for combat.</p><p>Yet one guy had my back again.</p><p>Taught me. Trained me. Was hard on me. But I needed it.</p><p>Years later, this Brother moves onto a new Path. And so do I.</p><p>I move to what would be my final assignment. Now I’m experienced. I’m looked up to. I have the knowledge, yet I still have much to learn.</p><p>I grow. I lead. I follow.</p><p>But this will be my last duty station. And it will be harder than ever to leave when I would rather stay.</p><p>I’m good at what I do. I love the mission. I love the adventure. Yet, there is an inner voice calling me somewhere else.</p><p>A different Path.</p><p>I must leave and head into yet another unknown. An even greater unknown.</p><p>I get out. My wife and I tour the USA for 3 months, then move to Panama.</p><p>I seek purpose. I seek what that yearning is. What is that inner voice leading me towards?</p><p>I find myself in Peru and meet another Brother on the Path.</p><p>We connect and start dancing with life.</p><p>Here I was (again), a newbie, needing elevated.</p><p>And yet again, I had someone to support me on this growth in a massive way.</p><p>We write together. We travel together. We advise together.</p><p>We do this for years and it’s a blast.</p><p>And then it happened again. I felt a different path calling. But it was much harder for me this time. I was stubborn. I thought I could have the best of both worlds.</p><p>This new thing with my current thing. Mashed together in what I desired to be harmoniously.</p><p>I resisted, resisted and resisted.</p><p>Until I couldn’t resist anymore.</p><p>I surrendered to the Path.</p><p>During this process, my heart hardened. Not wanting to leave this chapter, I blamed the Universe for the friction I felt inside.</p><p>Yet the whole time, this friction within was the gift of guidance.</p><p>I just didn’t wanna listen to it.</p><p>But eventually it made me listen. And once I shifted that, the pressure was released.</p><p>So here I am again, leaving a chapter, heading into an unknown to me.</p><p>Excited?</p><p>Absolutely.</p><p>Yet, sad that I have to leave.</p><p>Above all, I’m truly grateful.</p><p>Grateful for people on my Path who supported me in such profound and meaningful ways.</p><p>Grateful for such amazing experiences and the wisdom it brought me.</p><p>Grateful for a Path that keeps me on my toes, even if I’d rather have my feet firmly grounded.</p><p>What’s next for me?</p><p>I don’t know.</p><p>I do know that the essence of others helping me on my Path, is within me. And because it’s a part of me, I will share that with others.</p><p>This story has been how I received Life. I’ll share a giving story later.</p><p>I don’t know what’s next for me.</p><p>But I’m Present in the Now to find out.</p><p>Because, I’m sure it’s exactly what the moment calls for.</p>
